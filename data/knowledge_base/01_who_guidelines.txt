WHO Guidelines on Digital Screen Time and Adolescent Mental Health
Source: World Health Organization, 2019-2023

SECTION 1: RECOMMENDED SCREEN TIME LIMITS
The World Health Organization recommends that children and adolescents aged 5-17 should limit sedentary screen time to a maximum of 2 hours per day for recreational purposes. This includes social media use, video streaming, and gaming.

Key findings from WHO studies:
- Adolescents spending more than 3 hours daily on social media have a 2.1x higher risk of poor mental health outcomes
- Excessive screen time is associated with sleep disorders, anxiety, and depression
- Protective factors include physical activity, face-to-face social interactions, and adequate sleep

SECTION 2: SOCIAL MEDIA IMPACT
Social media platforms are designed to maximize engagement through dopamine feedback loops. For developing adolescent brains, this creates particular vulnerability:

1. Comparison Culture: Constant exposure to curated highlight reels of peers creates unrealistic expectations and social comparison anxiety
2. Cyberbullying Risk: 37% of adolescents report experiencing cyberbullying, with strong links to depression and suicidal ideation
3. Sleep Disruption: Blue light exposure and notification alerts disrupt circadian rhythms and reduce REM sleep quality
4. Fear of Missing Out (FOMO): Chronic anxiety from perceived social exclusion

SECTION 3: PROTECTIVE DIGITAL BEHAVIORS
Recommended healthy digital habits for adolescents:
- Use social media for less than 90 minutes per day
- Avoid screen use 1 hour before bedtime
- Disable push notifications
- Use grayscale screen mode to reduce addictive engagement
- Maintain at least one screen-free day per week

Risk classification thresholds based on WHO guidance:
- LOW RISK: Under 2 hours/day screen time, adequate sleep, regular exercise
- MEDIUM RISK: 2-4 hours/day, occasional sleep disruption, some anxiety symptoms
- HIGH RISK: Over 4 hours/day, chronic sleep deprivation, significant depression/anxiety symptoms
