Dataset Summary: Social Media Impact on Teen Mental Health
Source: Kaggle Dataset - algozee/teenager-menthal-healy (Apache 2.0 License)

SECTION 1: DATASET OVERVIEW
This dataset examines the relationship between social media usage and mental health outcomes in teenagers. It provides a comprehensive view of how digital habits correlate with psychological wellbeing.

Dataset specifications:
- Total records: Approximately 700+ samples
- Features: 13 columns (10 numeric, 2 categorical, 1 target)
- Target variable: mental_health_risk (Low / Medium / High)
- License: Apache 2.0 (free for academic and commercial use)
- Kaggle Usability Score: 10.0/10.0

SECTION 2: FEATURE DESCRIPTIONS

age (numeric):
- Adolescent's age in years
- Typical range: 13-19 years
- Importance: Brain development differs significantly across adolescence

gender (categorical: Male/Female/Other):
- Gender identity of the respondent
- Research shows gender differences in social media impact

social_media_hours (numeric):
- Average daily hours spent on social media platforms
- Key predictor variable
- Values > 4 hours associated with elevated mental health risk

sleep_hours (numeric):
- Average nightly sleep duration in hours
- Recommended: 8-10 hours for adolescents
- Strong predictor of mental health outcomes

physical_activity_hours (numeric):
- Weekly hours of physical activity
- Includes sports, gym, walking, etc.
- Protective factor for mental health

depression_score (numeric, 1-10 scale):
- Self-reported depression severity
- Higher score indicates more severe depression symptoms

anxiety_score (numeric, 1-10 scale):
- Self-reported anxiety level
- Higher score indicates more severe anxiety symptoms

stress_level (numeric, 1-10 scale):
- Perceived stress level from academic, social, and family sources

self_esteem_score (numeric, 1-10 scale):
- Measures self-worth and confidence
- Protective factor: higher scores associated with better mental health

academic_performance (categorical: Poor/Average/Good/Excellent):
- Self-reported school performance
- Links stress, mental health, and daily routine

online_support_access (binary: 0/1):
- Whether the adolescent has access to online mental health resources

family_support_score (numeric, 1-10 scale):
- Perceived family support level
- One of the strongest protective factors in the dataset

mental_health_risk (target - Low/Medium/High):
- 3-class classification target
- Based on combination of psychological scores and behavioral patterns

SECTION 3: KEY DATASET STATISTICS
Observed correlations:
- social_media_hours ↔ depression_score: Strong positive correlation
- sleep_hours ↔ anxiety_score: Moderate negative correlation
- physical_activity_hours ↔ stress_level: Moderate negative correlation
- family_support_score ↔ mental_health_risk: Strong negative correlation (high support = lower risk)

Class distribution (approximate):
- Low risk: ~33%
- Medium risk: ~40%
- High risk: ~27%

SECTION 4: ANALYSIS INSIGHTS
Top predictors of high mental health risk (based on feature importance):
1. depression_score (strongest predictor)
2. anxiety_score
3. social_media_hours (key behavioral predictor)
4. sleep_hours (sleep hygiene critical)
5. family_support_score (protective factor)
6. stress_level
7. self_esteem_score

Actionable interventions suggested by data:
- Reducing social media to under 2 hours/day has the strongest modifiable impact
- Improving sleep from 6 to 8 hours shows significant risk reduction
- Increasing weekly exercise by 3+ hours correlates with lower anxiety scores
- Family support interventions show outsized impact on risk reduction
