Dataset Summary: Social Media Impact on Teen Mental Health
Source: Kaggle Dataset - Teen Mental Health Dataset (Apache 2.0 License)

SECTION 1: DATASET OVERVIEW
This dataset examines the relationship between social media usage and mental health outcomes in teenagers. It provides a comprehensive view of how digital habits correlate with depression risk.

Dataset specifications:
- Total records: 1,200 samples
- Features: 13 columns (9 numeric, 3 categorical, 1 binary target)
- Target variable: depression_label (0=No Depression / 1=Depression Risk)
- Class distribution: 0 (Normal)=1169 (97.4%), 1 (Risk)=31 (2.6%) — SEVERE IMBALANCE
- License: Apache 2.0 (free for academic and commercial use)
- Kaggle Usability Score: 10.0/10.0

SECTION 2: FEATURE DESCRIPTIONS

age (numeric):
- Adolescent's age in years
- Typical range: 13-19 years
- Importance: Brain development differs significantly across adolescence

gender (categorical: male/female):
- Gender identity of the respondent
- Research shows gender differences in social media impact

daily_social_media_hours (numeric):
- Average daily hours spent on social media platforms
- Key predictor variable
- Values > 4 hours associated with elevated depression risk

platform_usage (categorical: Instagram/TikTok/Both):
- Primary social media platform used
- Different platforms may have different mental health impacts

sleep_hours (numeric):
- Average nightly sleep duration in hours
- Recommended: 8-10 hours for adolescents
- Strong predictor of mental health outcomes

screen_time_before_sleep (numeric):
- Hours of screen use within 1 hour before sleep
- Disrupts circadian rhythm and sleep quality

academic_performance (numeric, 1-10 scale):
- Self-reported academic performance score
- Links stress, mental health, and daily routine

physical_activity (numeric, hours/week):
- Weekly hours of physical activity
- Includes sports, gym, walking, etc.
- Protective factor for mental health

social_interaction_level (categorical: low/medium/high):
- Level of offline social interaction with peers
- Protective factor: higher interaction associated with better mental health

stress_level (numeric, 1-10 scale):
- Perceived stress level from academic, social, and family sources
- Higher score indicates more stress

anxiety_level (numeric, 1-10 scale):
- Self-reported anxiety level
- Higher score indicates more severe anxiety symptoms

addiction_level (numeric, 1-10 scale):
- Self-reported social media addiction severity
- Key behavioral predictor

depression_label (target - binary: 0/1):
- Binary classification target
- 0: No depression risk
- 1: Depression risk detected

SECTION 3: KEY DATASET STATISTICS
Observed correlations:
- daily_social_media_hours ↔ depression_label: Positive correlation
- sleep_hours ↔ anxiety_level: Moderate negative correlation
- physical_activity ↔ stress_level: Moderate negative correlation
- addiction_level ↔ depression_label: Strong positive correlation

Class distribution (actual):
- Normal (0): 1169 samples (97.4%)
- Depression Risk (1): 31 samples (2.6%)
- NOTE: Severe class imbalance — class_weight='balanced' required for all ML models

SECTION 4: ANALYSIS INSIGHTS
Top predictors of depression risk (based on feature importance):
1. anxiety_level (strongest predictor)
2. stress_level
3. addiction_level (key behavioral predictor)
4. daily_social_media_hours
5. sleep_hours (sleep hygiene critical)
6. screen_time_before_sleep
7. social_interaction_level (protective factor)

Actionable interventions suggested by data:
- Reducing social media addiction level is the most impactful modifiable factor
- Reducing daily social media to under 2 hours/day shows significant risk reduction
- Improving sleep quality (less screen time before sleep) correlates with lower anxiety
- Increasing offline social interactions is a strong protective factor
- Regular physical activity (3+ hours/week) reduces both stress and anxiety
